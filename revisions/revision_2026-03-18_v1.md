### 1.5 Definitions and Glossary

The following defined terms apply throughout this Manual unless a specific section expressly provides otherwise. Where a term is defined both here and in an Attachment, the Attachment definition governs for purposes of that Attachment.

**"Asset Management" or "Asset Mgmt"** means the Company's Asset Management Department, which is responsible for commercial lease negotiations, tenant and broker relationships, and leasing strategy.

**"Attachment A"** means the Delegation of Authority Matrix attached to this Manual, as amended from time to time, which sets forth approval thresholds and signatory authority for the Company's transactions and legal matters.

**"Authorized Persons"** means those individuals who have been granted delegated authority to act on behalf of the Company with respect to specified categories of transactions or decisions, as set forth in Attachment A (Delegation of Authority Matrix). References in this Manual to "certain authorized persons" shall be construed accordingly.

**"CEO"** means the Chief Executive Officer of the Company.

**"CFO"** means the Chief Financial Officer of the Company.

**"Company"** means PIR Industrial LLC and, unless the context requires otherwise, its subsidiaries and affiliated entities.

**"Department"** or **"Legal"** means the Legal Department of the Company, including all attorneys, paralegals, legal operations personnel, and administrative staff assigned to the Department.

**"DOA"** means the Delegation of Authority, as documented in Attachment A.

**"Entity Management System"** means the Company's purpose-built entity management platform (e.g., Diligent Entities, CSC Entity Management, or equivalent) designated as the single authoritative source for entity-related data, as described in Section 7.A.

**"GC"** or **"General Counsel"** means the General Counsel of the Company, who serves as the head of the Legal Department and the Company's chief legal officer. References to the "GC" include the GC's designee where expressly stated.

**"Legal Operations Coordinator"** means the Department staff member responsible for intake triage, Salesforce administration, and workflow coordination, or such other person as the GC may designate for those functions.

**"Legal Risk Tracker"** means the Department's centralized tracking tool for litigation, disputes, defaults, and other legal risk matters, as described in Section 10.

**"Material"** means, unless a specific dollar threshold is stated in the applicable section or Attachment, involving an amount, exposure, or value in excess of $250,000 (whether individually or in the aggregate for related matters). Where this Manual refers to "material" matters, transactions, or exposure without further specification, this threshold applies.

**"Near Term"** means within ninety (90) calendar days from the date of reference.

**"Periodically"** means, unless a more specific frequency is stated in the applicable section, no less frequently than quarterly (i.e., once per calendar quarter). Section-specific frequencies expressly stated elsewhere in this Manual (e.g., weekly, monthly, semi-annually, annually) override this default.

**"Salesforce"** means the Company's lease lifecycle management platform (currently Salesforce), used for intake, tracking, and reporting of leasing-related legal requests, as described in Section 4.

**"ShareFile"** means the Company's designated repository for executed legal documents and final transaction records. ShareFile is distinct from SharePoint.

**"SharePoint"** means the Company's designated platform for active document collaboration, version control, and centralized storage of draft legal documents and working files.

### Fix 4 -- Section 3: Environmental Regulatory Compliance (NEW subsection)

**Findings Addressed:** CA-13

#### Environmental Regulatory Compliance

The Legal Department provides legal oversight and support for the Company's environmental compliance obligations. As an owner and operator of industrial real estate, the Company is subject to federal, state, and local environmental laws and regulations, including but not limited to:

- **CERCLA (Comprehensive Environmental Response, Compensation, and Liability Act).** The Department monitors potential Superfund liability exposure, coordinates responses to EPA information requests or potentially responsible party (PRP) notifications, and advises on allocation and contribution claims. The Department coordinates with outside environmental counsel on any CERCLA matter involving potential liability to the Company.

- **RCRA (Resource Conservation and Recovery Act).** The Department advises on compliance with hazardous waste generation, storage, and disposal requirements applicable to the Company's properties, including tenant activities that may create RCRA obligations for the Company as property owner.

- **Phase I and Phase II Environmental Site Assessments.** The Department coordinates with the Company's environmental consultants to ensure that Phase I Environmental Site Assessments (in accordance with ASTM E1527) are completed for all acquisitions and, where recommended, Phase II assessments are conducted. The Department reviews all environmental reports for legal sufficiency, including evaluation of recognized environmental conditions (RECs), and advises the Acquisitions Department and executive leadership on environmental risk.

- **Environmental Indemnification in Leases.** The Department ensures that the Company's standard lease forms contain appropriate environmental representations, warranties, covenants, and indemnification provisions. For industrial tenants whose operations involve hazardous materials, the Department reviews and approves tenant environmental disclosure schedules and coordinates insurance requirements (e.g., environmental impairment liability policies) where appropriate.

- **State and Local Environmental Requirements.** The Department monitors compliance with state-specific environmental disclosure, remediation, and reporting requirements (e.g., New Jersey ISRA, Connecticut Transfer Act, Illinois SRP) applicable to the Company's properties.

- **Environmental Incident Response.** Upon notification of an environmental incident (e.g., spill, release, contamination discovery) at a Company property, the Department coordinates the Company's legal response, including notification to applicable regulatory authorities, engagement of environmental counsel, preservation of indemnification and insurance claims, and communication with affected tenants and third parties.

The Department maintains an Environmental Risk Log as a component of the Legal Risk Tracker (Section 10) to track all properties with known or suspected environmental conditions, pending regulatory actions, and active remediation obligations.

### Fix 13 -- Section 5: Privilege Preservation in Collaborative Drafting (NEW subsection)

**Findings Addressed:** AR-02

**Instruction:** ADD the following as a new subsection in Section 5, immediately after the "Tracked Changes Protocol" subsection.

#### Privilege Preservation in Collaborative Drafting

To protect attorney-client privilege during the collaborative drafting process, the following rules apply:

**Legal advice must not be provided within lease draft documents.** Department attorneys shall not provide legal analysis, risk assessments, strategic recommendations, or other legal advice through comments, @mentions, or annotations embedded within lease drafts or other transactional documents stored in SharePoint or any other shared workspace. In-document comments by attorneys must be limited to objective drafting instructions (e.g., "insert tenant name," "confirm square footage," "update rent commencement date").

Legal advice -- including analysis of risk, recommendations regarding negotiating posture, assessments of enforceability, or evaluations of legal exposure -- must be communicated through separate privileged channels, including:

- Emails clearly marked "PRIVILEGED AND CONFIDENTIAL -- ATTORNEY-CLIENT COMMUNICATION"
- Privileged memoranda stored separately from the transactional document workspace
- Verbal communications (with follow-up documented in a privileged writing where appropriate)

This separation is required because comments and @mentions embedded in shared documents are discoverable in litigation and may be deemed a waiver of privilege if they contain substantive legal advice. The Department shall include this guidance in its quarterly training for Asset Management (see Section 11).

### Fix 14 -- Section 5: Draft Version Numbering and Internal Draft Protection

**Findings Addressed:** AR-18, AR-20

**Instruction:** REPLACE the existing "Document Naming Convention" subsection content in Section 5 with the following:

#### Document Naming Convention

All draft lease documents must follow the Company's standardized naming convention to ensure consistency and clarity.

The standard naming format is:

**[Document Type] -- [Tenant Name] -- [Property Address] -- PLYM Draft**

Subsequent versions circulated externally shall be numbered sequentially beginning with "v1" for each new external circulation. Examples:

*Lease -- ABC Logistics -- 100 Industrial Drive -- PLYM Draft v1*
*Lease -- ABC Logistics -- 100 Industrial Drive -- PLYM Draft v2*

**Internal vs. External Version Tracking.** Internal working drafts shall use non-sequential, date-based identifiers (e.g., "PLYM Internal 2026-03-15") rather than sequential version numbers. This practice prevents external parties from inferring the number of internal revisions or the existence of undisclosed internal drafts. Upon determination that a draft is ready for external circulation, the document shall be saved under the standard naming convention with a sequential external version number (v1, v2, etc.) that is independent of the internal version history.

**Post-Execution Document Retention.** After a lease is fully executed, only the final executed version and the versions that were actually circulated externally need be retained in the primary transaction folder. Internal working drafts should be moved to a separate archive subfolder within SharePoint, accessible only to the Department, and may be disposed of in accordance with the document retention schedule (Section 8.C). Only the externally circulated versions and the final executed document shall be retained in the deal team's shared workspace.

Consistent naming conventions help prevent confusion and allow the deal team to easily identify the most recent version of a document.

### 7. Corporate Governance & Entity Management

The Legal Department is responsible for overseeing the Company's corporate governance framework, maintaining accurate and complete records for all legal entities within the Company's organizational structure, ensuring continuous compliance with the Internal Revenue Code ("IRC") requirements for real estate investment trust ("REIT") qualification under IRC Sections 856 through 860, and coordinating with Finance and external advisors on all SEC reporting obligations. These procedures are designed to satisfy the governance, compliance, and reporting standards expected by the Company's credit facility lenders, joint venture partners, and regulatory authorities.

These procedures are intended to:

- Ensure continuous compliance with REIT qualification requirements under IRC Sections 856-860
- Maintain accurate records of entity structure, authority, and ownership across all jurisdictions
- Support financing, transactional, and operational needs, including credit facility covenant compliance
- Preserve institutional knowledge of the Company's legal structure
- Coordinate with Finance on SEC periodic reporting obligations (Forms 10-K, 10-Q, and 8-K)
- Provide a framework for internal reporting of compliance concerns, including whistleblower protections

#### A. Entity Management

The Legal Department maintains a centralized record of all Company entities in the Company's dedicated entity management system ("Entity Management System"). The Entity Management System shall be a purpose-built platform (e.g., Diligent Entities, CSC Entity Management, or equivalent) designated as the single authoritative source for entity-related data. The Entity Management System must, at a minimum, capture and maintain current records for each entity, including:

- Legal name and any d/b/a names
- Jurisdiction of formation and date of formation
- Entity type (LLC, LP, corporation, statutory trust, etc.)
- Current registered agent and registered office address in each jurisdiction of formation and qualification
- Good standing status in each jurisdiction of formation and qualification
- Jurisdictions in which the entity is qualified to do business (foreign qualifications)
- Current managers, officers, directors, or other governing persons, with effective dates
- Ownership structure, including all equity holders, membership interests, partnership interests, or share ownership, with percentage interests
- Governing documents (operating agreements, partnership agreements, bylaws, certificates of formation/incorporation, amendments) with version history
- Taxpayer identification number (EIN)
- Fiscal year end

The Legal Department conducts a comprehensive annual review of all entity records in the Entity Management System no later than the end of the first quarter of each calendar year. The annual review must confirm the accuracy of all data fields listed above and identify any entities that require dissolution, withdrawal, or restructuring. The results of the annual review are documented in a written memorandum from the GC to the CEO and CFO, identifying any discrepancies corrected and any recommended actions.

#### B. State Filing Compliance

The Legal Department, in coordination with the Company's registered agent service provider, is responsible for monitoring and ensuring timely compliance with all state filing obligations for each Company entity, including:

- Annual reports and statements of information required by each jurisdiction of formation and qualification
- Franchise tax filings and payments
- Foreign qualification filings for entities conducting business in new jurisdictions
- Amendments to formation documents filed with the applicable Secretary of State or equivalent authority
- Registered agent and registered office changes
- Withdrawal filings for entities no longer conducting business in a jurisdiction

The Department maintains a State Filing Calendar in the Entity Management System (or, if the Entity Management System does not support calendar functionality, in a dedicated compliance calendar maintained in SharePoint). The State Filing Calendar tracks all filing deadlines, responsible persons, and completion status. The GC reviews the State Filing Calendar no less frequently than monthly.

Missed or late state filings must be escalated to the GC within one (1) business day of discovery. The GC determines the appropriate remedial action, which may include expedited filing, reinstatement, or engagement of outside counsel in the applicable jurisdiction.

#### C. REIT Compliance Monitoring

The Company has elected to be treated as a real estate investment trust ("REIT") under IRC Sections 856 through 860. Continuous compliance with REIT qualification requirements is essential to the Company's tax-advantaged structure. The Legal Department, in coordination with Finance and the Company's outside tax counsel and auditors, is responsible for monitoring and supporting compliance with the following REIT requirements:

**Asset Tests (IRC Section 856(c)(4)).** The Legal Department shall coordinate with Finance to monitor compliance with the REIT asset tests on a quarterly basis, including: (i) at least 75% of total assets must be real estate assets, cash, cash items, and government securities; (ii) not more than 25% of total assets may be securities other than those qualifying under clause (i); (iii) not more than 5% of total assets may consist of securities of any one issuer (other than a TRS or qualifying REIT); and (iv) the Company may not hold more than 10% of the outstanding voting securities or 10% of the total value of the outstanding securities of any one issuer (other than a TRS or qualifying REIT).

**Gross Income Tests (IRC Section 856(c)(2) and (3)).** The Legal Department shall coordinate with Finance to monitor compliance with the REIT gross income tests on a quarterly basis, including: (i) at least 75% of gross income must be derived from rents from real property, interest on obligations secured by mortgages on real property, and other qualifying real estate income; and (ii) at least 95% of gross income must be derived from qualifying real estate income plus dividends, interest, and gain from the sale or disposition of stock or securities.

The Legal Department shall coordinate with Finance to ensure that rents from real property satisfy the requirements of IRC Section 856(d), including limitations on impermissible tenant services, related-party rents, and personal property rent components exceeding 15% of total rent.

**Distribution Requirements (IRC Section 857(a)).** The Legal Department shall coordinate with Finance to monitor compliance with the REIT distribution requirement, which requires the Company to distribute annually at least 90% of its REIT taxable income (determined without regard to the dividends paid deduction and excluding net capital gains).

The Legal Department shall coordinate with Finance to verify that distribution calculations are completed no later than sixty (60) days after the close of each taxable year and that required distributions are declared and paid within the time limits prescribed by IRC Section 857.

**Organizational Requirements (IRC Section 856(a) and (b)).** The Legal Department shall verify, on an annual basis, that the Company continues to satisfy the REIT organizational requirements, including: (i) the Company is managed by one or more trustees or directors; (ii) beneficial ownership is evidenced by transferable shares or certificates; (iii) the Company would be taxable as a domestic corporation but for Sections 856-860; (iv) the Company is not a financial institution or insurance company; and (v) the Company satisfies the 100-shareholders test and the closely-held prohibition (5/50 rule) under IRC Section 856(a)(5) and (6).

#### D. SEC Reporting Coordination

The Legal Department coordinates with Finance and outside securities counsel on all SEC periodic reporting obligations, including Forms 10-K, 10-Q, and 8-K. The Department's responsibilities include:

- Reviewing and providing legal sign-off on all SEC filings before submission
- Coordinating with outside securities counsel on disclosure obligations
- Monitoring SEC rule changes and their impact on the Company's reporting obligations
- Supporting the preparation of the Management's Discussion and Analysis section of periodic reports
- Coordinating with Finance on internal controls over financial reporting (ICFR) disclosures

#### E. Whistleblower and Internal Reporting

The Company maintains a mechanism for internal reporting of compliance concerns, including potential violations of law, regulation, or Company policy. The Legal Department is responsible for:

- Maintaining a confidential reporting channel (hotline or web-based portal) for employees and stakeholders
- Investigating reports of compliance concerns in coordination with Human Resources and, where appropriate, outside counsel
- Protecting reporters from retaliation in accordance with applicable whistleblower protection statutes (including SOX Section 806, if applicable)
- Documenting all reports, investigations, and outcomes in a confidential log maintained in the Privileged Legal Site
- Reporting aggregated whistleblower data (number of reports, categories, resolution status) to the Board on a quarterly basis, without identifying individual reporters unless required by law

#### F. Governance Calendar

The Department maintains a Governance Calendar to track all governance-related deadlines and recurring obligations, including:

- Board and committee meeting schedules
- State filing deadlines (annual reports, franchise taxes, foreign qualifications)
- REIT compliance testing deadlines (quarterly asset tests, income tests, distribution calculations)
- SEC filing deadlines (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K)
- Entity management annual review
- Insurance policy renewal dates
- Credit facility covenant compliance dates
- Registered agent renewals

The Governance Calendar is maintained in the Entity Management System or SharePoint and reviewed by the GC monthly.

#### G. Organizational Chart

The Department maintains a current organizational chart reflecting the Company's entity structure, ownership percentages, and governing relationships. The organizational chart is updated within ten (10) business days of any change in entity structure and is stored in the Entity Management System and SharePoint. A current copy is provided to Finance, outside auditors, and credit facility lenders upon request.

### 8. Confidentiality & Information Handling

The Legal Department handles sensitive and privileged information across multiple workstreams. This Section establishes the confidentiality standards, privilege preservation requirements, and information handling procedures that apply to all Department personnel and to any non-Department personnel who access or receive confidential legal information.

#### A. Classification of Confidential Information

All information handled by the Department is classified into the following categories:

- **Privileged (Attorney-Client / Work Product):** Communications made for the purpose of seeking or providing legal advice, and materials prepared in anticipation of litigation or at the direction of counsel. These materials must bear the designation: "PRIVILEGED AND CONFIDENTIAL -- ATTORNEY-CLIENT COMMUNICATION" or "PRIVILEGED AND CONFIDENTIAL -- ATTORNEY WORK PRODUCT."
- **Confidential -- Business Sensitive:** Non-privileged information that is proprietary, commercially sensitive, or subject to contractual confidentiality obligations.
- **Internal Use Only:** Information intended for Company personnel but not subject to heightened confidentiality protections.

#### B. Privilege Preservation Requirements

All Department personnel must comply with the following privilege preservation requirements:

- Privileged communications must be clearly marked with the applicable privilege designation. Privilege designations are mandatory, not optional.
- Privileged materials must be stored in the Privileged Legal Site within SharePoint (see Section 8.5(H) for SharePoint segmentation requirements).
- Distribution of privileged materials must be limited to individuals with a need to know: Department personnel, the GC, specifically authorized executive leadership, and engaged outside counsel.
- Inadvertent disclosure of privileged materials must be reported to the GC immediately upon discovery for assessment of remedial measures (including clawback demands under FRE 502(b) or equivalent state rules).

#### C. Confidentiality Obligations

All Company personnel who receive or access confidential legal information are subject to confidentiality obligations. The Department enforces these obligations through:

- Confidentiality acknowledgments signed by all Department personnel upon hire and annually thereafter
- Confidentiality provisions in engagement letters with outside counsel
- Confidentiality provisions in vendor agreements for any vendor with access to legal information
- Training on confidentiality obligations as part of the Department's training program (Section 11)

Document retention periods and destruction procedures are governed by Section 8.5 of this Manual. All personnel must comply with applicable litigation holds, which supersede any retention schedule (see Section 8.5(C)).

#### D. Fair Housing and ADA Compliance Records

The Department maintains appropriate confidentiality protections for records related to Fair Housing Act and Americans with Disabilities Act compliance, including:

- Maintaining records of all fair housing and ADA complaints, investigations, and resolutions
- Ensuring that all fair housing and ADA complaint records are retained in accordance with the retention schedule in Section 8.5(A) and are stored in access-restricted SharePoint folders
- Coordinating with Property Management and Human Resources on fair housing and ADA training requirements

### 8.5 Document Retention & Litigation Hold Procedures

These procedures are designed to ensure compliance with applicable legal and regulatory obligations, to preserve the Company's ability to defend its interests in litigation, and to protect against spoliation claims.

#### A. Document Retention Schedule

#### B. Electronic Records Management

The Company generates and receives electronic records across multiple platforms. The following requirements apply to the management of all electronically stored information ("ESI"):

- **Email.** All business email is retained in the Company's email archiving system for the retention period specified in Section 8.5(A). Employees must not delete emails from the archive system. Personal email accounts must not be used for Company business.
- **Microsoft Teams.** All Teams messages, including direct messages and channel conversations, are captured by the Company's compliance archiving solution and retained per the schedule in Section 8.5(A). Employees must not use personal messaging applications for Company business communications.
- **Text Messages (SMS/iMessage).** Business-related text messages must be captured using the Company's approved mobile archiving solution. Employees who use personal devices for Company business must enroll in the Company's mobile device management (MDM) program or approved archiving application.
- **Cloud Storage.** Company documents must be stored only in approved cloud platforms (SharePoint, ShareFile). Use of personal cloud storage (Dropbox, Google Drive, iCloud, OneDrive personal) for Company business is prohibited.
- **Voicemail.** Business voicemails are retained per the schedule in Section 8.5(A). The IT Department is responsible for configuring voicemail retention settings in accordance with this policy.

The Legal Department, in coordination with IT, must conduct an annual audit of electronic records management compliance to verify that archiving systems are functioning as required and that retention settings are properly configured.

#### C. Litigation Hold Procedures

Upon identification of a matter that reasonably may result in litigation, arbitration, governmental investigation, or regulatory inquiry, the Legal Department must implement a litigation hold to preserve all potentially relevant documents, communications, and ESI. A litigation hold supersedes all retention schedules and destruction protocols set forth in this Manual.

**C.1. First 48 Hours -- Litigation Response Checklist.** Upon identification of a litigation trigger event, the following actions must be completed within forty-eight (48) hours:

1. The General Counsel (or designee) identifies all potentially relevant custodians, data sources, and document repositories.
2. The General Counsel (or designee) drafts and issues a written Litigation Hold Notice to all identified custodians, specifying: (a) the matter giving rise to the hold; (b) the categories of documents and ESI to be preserved; (c) the custodian's obligation to preserve all potentially relevant materials, including materials on personal devices and in personal cloud storage; and (d) the prohibition on destruction, alteration, or deletion of any potentially relevant materials.
3. The Legal Department submits a Litigation Hold -- IT Coordination Form (see Section 8.5(F)) to the IT Department, requesting suspension of all automated deletion, archiving, or overwrite processes for data within the scope of the hold.
4. The Legal Department notifies engaged outside counsel (if any) of the hold and coordinates on preservation scope.
5. The General Counsel notifies executive leadership as required by Section 9 (Communication & Escalation Protocols).
6. The Legal Department opens an entry in the Litigation Hold Log (see Section 8.5(G)), recording: hold identification number, matter name, date of issuance, custodian list, and scope of preservation.

**C.2. Custodian Acknowledgment.** Each custodian who receives a Litigation Hold Notice must acknowledge receipt in writing (email acknowledgment is sufficient) within seventy-two (72) hours of issuance.

**C.3. Escalation for Non-Acknowledgment.** If a custodian does not acknowledge within seventy-two (72) hours:

1. The Legal Department issues a second written notice to the custodian, copying the custodian's direct supervisor.
2. If no acknowledgment is received within an additional forty-eight (48) hours, the Legal Department escalates to the custodian's department head and Human Resources.
3. If the custodian remains non-responsive, the General Counsel directs IT to implement technical preservation measures (e.g., imaging the custodian's devices, placing a hold on the custodian's email account) and documents the non-compliance for potential disciplinary action.

**C.4. Quarterly Re-Certification.** For all active litigation holds, the Legal Department must conduct a quarterly re-certification process, issuing Re-Certification Notices to all custodians and documenting compliance status in the Litigation Hold Log.

**C.5. Scope of Preservation -- Personal Devices and Cloud Storage.** Litigation holds must encompass all potentially relevant materials regardless of storage location, including Company systems, Company-issued devices, personal devices used for Company business, personal cloud storage accounts, hard-copy documents, and backup tapes.

**C.6. Integration with Retention Schedule.** An active litigation hold supersedes all retention schedule provisions for documents within the scope of the hold. The Legal Department must cross-reference the Litigation Hold Log against the retention schedule before authorizing any document destruction.

#### D. Hold Release and Document Destruction

**D.1. Hold Release.** A litigation hold may be released only upon written authorization of the General Counsel, following confirmation that the underlying matter has been fully resolved and all applicable appeal periods have expired.

**D.2. Document Destruction Procedures.** Documents that have reached the end of their applicable retention period (and are not subject to any active litigation hold) must be destroyed in accordance with documented procedures, including GC-approved Destruction Authorization Memoranda and Certificates of Destruction.

#### E. Privacy and Data Protection

The Company's document retention and destruction practices must comply with all applicable federal, state, and local privacy and data protection laws, including CCPA/CPRA, state data breach notification laws, state data disposal laws, HIPAA (to the extent applicable), and FCRA (to the extent applicable).

#### F. Legal-to-IT Litigation Hold Coordination

All litigation hold implementation and release actions requiring IT support must be submitted via the Litigation Hold -- IT Coordination Form, with defined SLAs (4 business hours for acknowledgment, 24 hours for Urgent requests, 48 hours for Standard requests).

#### G. Litigation Hold Audit Trail

The Legal Department must maintain a comprehensive Litigation Hold Log in a secure, access-restricted location within the Privileged Legal Site.

#### H. SharePoint Segmentation for Privileged Materials

All privileged, litigation-related, and attorney work product materials must be stored in a segregated SharePoint site collection ("Privileged Legal Site") that is separate from the Company's general SharePoint environment. Access is restricted to Legal Department personnel, the GC, and specifically authorized executive leadership on a documented need-to-know basis. Outside counsel access is limited to matter-specific document libraries with time-limited permissions.

#### I. Outside Counsel Access Controls

Outside counsel may be granted access to relevant documents stored in the Privileged Legal Site, subject to matter-specific, time-limited, GC-approved permissions. Upon conclusion of the engagement, all access must be revoked within five (5) business days.

### 9. Communication & Escalation Protocols

Effective legal risk management requires clear communication channels and timely escalation of matters that may affect the Company's legal, financial, or operational interests. This section establishes when and how matters must be communicated to and escalated within the Legal Department and to executive leadership. All timelines in this section are intended as guidelines to promote prompt action; the overriding obligation is to communicate and escalate as soon as practicable under the circumstances.

#### A. Routine Communication and Interdepartmental Coordination

Standing interdepartmental meetings are held at the following cadence:

| Meeting | Participants | Frequency | Trigger for Ad Hoc Session | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Leasing Pipeline Review | Legal + Asset Mgmt | Weekly (scheduled) | New lease request exceeding $[X] annual rent; lease dispute notice | Review pending lease requests, priority alignment, negotiation strategy |
| Litigation/Risk Update | Legal + Executive Leadership | Bi-weekly (scheduled) | New litigation filed; matter exceeding Significant risk threshold | Active matters, exposure updates, strategic decisions |

#### B. Escalation Criteria

Matters must be escalated to the Legal Department promptly when they involve potential or actual litigation, demand letters, tenant bankruptcy, financial exposure exceeding $50,000, regulatory inquiries, significant personal injury or death, property damage exceeding $25,000, confidentiality breaches, environmental incidents, insurance disputes, or media inquiries involving legal issues.

#### C. Escalation Timelines and Response Actions

| Event Type | Escalation Timeline | Escalate To | Required Response Actions |
|---|---|---|---|
| Receipt of lawsuit | Same business day | General Counsel | GC reviews filing; identifies response deadline; engages outside counsel if warranted; initiates litigation hold; notifies insurance carrier |
| Demand letter or threat of litigation | Same business day | General Counsel | GC evaluates merit and exposure; prepares preliminary assessment; determines outside counsel engagement; initiates litigation hold if warranted |
| Regulatory inquiry, subpoena, or investigation | Same business day | General Counsel + CEO | GC reviews scope; identifies response deadline; initiates litigation hold; engages regulatory counsel; prepares board notification if material |
| Tenant bankruptcy filing | Next business day | General Counsel + Asset Mgmt Lead | GC reviews petition; identifies deadlines for proofs of claim; coordinates with Asset Mgmt; engages bankruptcy counsel |
| Significant personal injury or death | Immediately | General Counsel + Property Mgmt Lead | GC initiates investigation coordination; directs scene preservation; notifies insurance carrier; initiates litigation hold |
| Financial exposure exceeding $500K | Next business day | General Counsel + CFO | GC prepares preliminary exposure assessment; coordinates reserve adjustment; evaluates insurance coverage |

#### D. Escalation Flowchart

1. Originating Department Contact identifies the event and immediately notifies the Legal Department.
2. General Counsel evaluates the matter and determines appropriate response actions.
3. If the matter involves potential financial exposure exceeding $500,000, reputational risk, portfolio-wide implications, or regulatory action, the GC notifies the CEO.
4. If the matter involves financial exposure or reserve implications, the GC notifies the CFO.
5. If the matter meets the threshold for board notification, the GC prepares a board notification memorandum.
6. If the GC is unavailable, the originating contact must notify the CEO directly.

#### E. Executive Escalation Memoranda

When escalation to executive leadership is required, the Legal Department must prepare a written escalation memorandum containing: matter identification, summary of facts, preliminary evaluation of legal exposure, applicable insurance coverage, recommended course of action, applicable deadlines, and outside counsel status. Escalation memoranda must be marked "PRIVILEGED AND CONFIDENTIAL -- ATTORNEY-CLIENT COMMUNICATION."

#### F. Consequence Framework for Missed Escalations

The Legal Department monitors escalation compliance and addresses deficiencies through quarterly reporting, CEO/Board notification for escalation failures resulting in actual or potential prejudice, performance management for repeated failures, and annual escalation protocol training.

The Legal Department provides periodic updates to executive leadership through:

- **Monthly CFO Report:** Reserve reconciliation, legal spend analysis, insurance claim status

### 10. Legal Risk Tracking & Reporting

The Legal Department maintains a Legal Risk Tracker and Legal Risk Dashboard to provide structured visibility into legal matters affecting the Company's portfolio. All risk assessments, exposure estimates, and related analyses are preliminary evaluations prepared at the direction of counsel for the purpose of providing legal advice.

#### A. Risk Categories

Legal matters are categorized into: Litigation, Tenant Defaults, Bankruptcy, Insurance Claims, Regulatory Matters, Environmental, Employment, Contract Disputes (Non-Lease), Corporate/Governance, and Data Privacy/Security.

#### B. Risk Assessment Methodology

Each matter is evaluated using a five-tier probability-impact framework:

| Rating | Probability of Adverse Outcome | Gross Financial Impact Range | Update Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Critical | Probable (> 80%) | > $2,000,000 | Weekly (or upon any material development) |
| High | Likely (60% - 80%) | $500,000 - $2,000,000 | Bi-weekly |
| Significant | Possible (40% - 60%) | $150,000 - $500,000 | Monthly |
| Moderate | Unlikely (20% - 40%) | $50,000 - $150,000 | Monthly |
| Low | Remote (< 20%) | $0 - $50,000 | Quarterly |

Risk rating changes must be approved by the General Counsel with documented rationale, recorded in the Legal Risk Tracker, and communicated to executive leadership.

#### C. Probability-Weighted Expected Loss Methodology

For each matter rated Moderate or above, the Legal Department must calculate a probability-weighted expected loss ("PWEL") to inform reserve recommendations, following a structured methodology: identify potential outcomes, assign probability weights, estimate financial impact, calculate PWEL, determine net PWEL (after insurance), and document assumptions.

#### D. Gross vs. Net Exposure Tracking

The Legal Risk Tracker must separately track gross exposure, insurance recovery estimates, other offsets, and net exposure for each matter. Net exposure figures must be clearly distinguished from gross exposure in all reports.

#### E. Reserve Reconciliation with Finance

Monthly reserve reviews and quarterly formal reconciliation meetings between the GC and CFO. Reserve adjustments exceeding $50,000 require written concurrence from both. Adjustments exceeding $250,000 require CEO notification. Adjustments exceeding $1,000,000 require Board notification.

#### F. Insurance Program Integration

The Legal Department must maintain current knowledge of the Company's insurance program and integrate insurance considerations into all risk tracking and reporting activities, including coverage analysis for each new matter and tracking of insurer reserving positions.

#### G. Reporting Cadence and Dashboard Distribution

Monthly Dashboard distributed to GC, CEO, and CFO. Quarterly comprehensive Legal Risk Report to the Board. Ad hoc reports for Critical matters. All dashboards and risk reports are privileged and subject to controlled distribution.

#### H. Use of Risk Data

Risk data informs reserve decisions, strategic planning, proactive risk mitigation, standard form improvements, outside counsel performance evaluation, and insurance program renewals.

PRIVILEGED AND CONFIDENTIAL -- PREPARED AT DIRECTION OF COUNSEL

#### A.1. Purpose

This Attachment establishes the delegation of approval authority for legal, transactional, and operational matters. All dollar thresholds are subject to annual review and adjustment by the GC and CEO.

#### A.2. Approval Tiers

Five tiers:

- **Tier 1 -- Paralegal / Senior Paralegal:** Routine administrative and procedural actions. No counter-signatory required.
- **Tier 2 -- Associate General Counsel:** Actions within specified dollar thresholds. Documented approval required.
- **Tier 3 -- General Counsel:** GC approval with counter-signatory (CFO). GC may not self-approve without counter-signatory.
- **Tier 4 -- General Counsel + CEO:** Documented approval by both required.
- **Tier 5 -- General Counsel + CEO + Board:** Board ratification or approval required.

Tier-specific documentation requirements from Tier 1 (Salesforce record) through Tier 5 (Board minutes or written consent).

#### A.5. Conflict of Interest Provision

Individuals with conflicts must recuse and escalate to next higher Tier. Conflicts disclosed to GC (or CEO if GC is conflicted). Confidential recusal log maintained.

#### A.6. Emergency Authority

- GC unavailable: CFO may exercise Tier 3 for time-sensitive matters
- GC and CEO unavailable: COO may exercise Tier 3
- 72-hour time limit per exercise
- No emergency authority for Tier 5 (Board) matters
- Ratification required within 5 business days

#### A.7. Settlement Approval Procedures

Matters exceeding $100,000 require approval before terms communicated to opposing party. Time-sensitive settlement conditional approval via email with 48-hour formal ratification requirement.

#### A.8. Annual Review

Reviewed annually with Manual review. Dollar thresholds evaluated against portfolio size, transaction volume, and risk tolerance.

#### C.1. Rate Caps (POPULATED)

| Timekeeper Level | Maximum Hourly Rate |
|---|---|
| Partner | $650.00 |
| Senior Associate (4+ years) | $475.00 |
| Junior Associate (< 4 years) | $350.00 |
| Paralegal / Legal Assistant | $225.00 |

Annual rate increases capped at 3% with 60-day advance notice. Increases exceeding 3% require GC pre-approval.

#### C.2. Staffing Requirements

Lean staffing, responsible partner identified, work delegated to lowest-cost competent timekeeper, max 2 attorneys per deposition/hearing/meeting, no summer/first-year associates without approval, new staffing requires GC pre-approval, transition billing is non-billable.

#### C.3. Billing Format and Requirements

LEDES 1998B format required via e-billing platform. Monthly submission within 45 days. 0.1-hour increments. No block billing. Detailed descriptions required. No single entry exceeding 0.5 hours without detailed description.

#### C.4. Daily Hour Caps (NEW)

Maximum 10 hours per day per timekeeper. Company reserves right to request total daily hours certification.

#### C.5. Intra-Firm Conference Limits (NEW)

Maximum 2 attorneys billable per internal conference. Capped at 0.5 hours per conference per timekeeper. Routine supervisory review not separately billable.

#### C.6. Prohibited Charges

Administrative overhead, database access fees (except targeted research with GC pre-approval), first-class/business-class airfare, alcohol, internal meetings/training, unauthorized rush charges, firm technology infrastructure, interest/late fees, meals exceeding GSA per diem.

#### C.7. Travel

Pre-approval required. Economy class. GSA per diem rates. Travel time at 50% of hourly rate. IRS mileage rate. Receipts required for expenses exceeding $25.

#### C.8. Expense Pre-Approval

Expert witnesses, travel/lodging, outsourced discovery, filing fees exceeding $1,000, any single expense exceeding $2,500.

#### C.9. Matter Budgets

Phase-based budget required at outset. 15% overrun triggers written notice and revised budget requirement (violation if not obtained). Quarterly budget-to-actual reports.

#### C.10. Reporting Requirements

Monthly status reports by 15th of month. Prompt notification within 24 hours of material developments. Early case assessment within 30 days. Failure to report triggers automatic invoice processing hold. GC maintains reporting deadline calendar.

#### C.11. Invoice Review and Payment

Subject to segregation of duties (Section 11.F.2). Payment terms: net 45 days (revised from 60 days). Non-compliant invoices returned. GC determination is final for disputes unresolved within 30 days.

#### C.12. Audit Rights (NEW)

Company reserves right to audit billing records with 30 days' notice. 3-year records retention requirement. Full cooperation required. Systematic overbilling (>5% adjustments) permits cost recovery.

#### C.13. Panel Selection and Diversity Requirements (NEW)

Annual panel review with documented criteria (expertise, geographic coverage, quality, rates, diversity, billing compliance, conflicts). Competitive bidding for matters exceeding $100,000 estimated fees. Diversity requirements including annual data, meaningful staffing, and annual CEO reporting.

#### C.14. Non-Compliance Escalation (NEW)

Graduated: First violation (written notice) -> Second violation (adjustment + warning) -> Third violation (panel removal consideration) -> Immediate termination for egregious violations.

#### C.15. Conflicts and Confidentiality

Ongoing disclosure obligation. Confidentiality requirements. No public statements without GC consent. Return/destroy Company materials upon termination of engagement.

#### C.16. Representations and Warranties Support (NEW)

Outside counsel must provide written summary of material exceptions, risks, and recommended bring-down conditions for transactional reps and warranties, delivered at least 5 business days before closing.

#### C.17. Acknowledgment

Signed acknowledgment required within 10 business days. Continued performance constitutes acceptance.
